Organizational Structure of Indian Air Force

Air Headquarters

Air Headquarters (Vayu Bhawan) is the top-level structure where the Chief of the Air Staff (CAS) and senior officers make strategic decisions. The CAS is assisted by Vice Chief of the Air Staff (VCAS) and Deputy Chiefs of the Air Staff (DCAS), along with several Principal Staff Officers (PSOs) who oversee different branches such as operations, maintenance, administration, and training.

Operational Commands

  • Western Air Command: New Delhi - Northern and Western India
  • Eastern Air Command: Shillong - Eastern India
  • Central Air Command: Prayagraj - Central India
  • Southern Air Command: Thiruvananthapuram - Southern India
  • South Western Air Command: Gandhinagar - Western and Southwestern India

Functional Commands

  • Training Command: Bangalore - Training and education of IAF personnel
  • Maintenance Command: Nagpur - Maintenance and logistics support

Hierarchy

Officer Ranks

  • Air Chief Marshal: Chief of the Air Staff, overall command of the IAF
  • Air Marshal: Senior commands and strategic planning
  • Air Vice Marshal: Commands Air Force stations and groups
  • Air Commodore: Commands wings and stations

More Officer Ranks

  • Group Captain: Commands squadrons and airbases
  • Wing Commander: Commands wings and squadrons
  • Squadron Leader: Commands flights within squadrons
  • Flight Lieutenant: Leads smaller units or assists higher command
  • Flying Officer: Entry-level officer, commands junior personnel

Airmen Ranks

  • Master Warrant Officer: Senior enlisted advisor and experienced technician
  • Warrant Officer: Leads large groups of airmen and provides technical expertise
  • Junior Warrant Officer: Assists Warrant Officers and supervises airmen
  • Sergeant: Supervises and trains junior airmen

Maintenance and Logistics Support

The Indian Air Force has specialized units dedicated to maintaining the highest standards of operational readiness for its fleet. Maintenance training ensures that ground crew and technicians are well-equipped with the knowledge to handle technical challenges. The logistical support network plays a crucial role in ensuring smooth operations and quick responses during missions.

Key Areas of Focus:

  • Technical Training: Focused on aircraft, radar, and weapons systems maintenance.
  • Ground Support: Ensuring aircraft readiness with frequent checks and necessary repairs.
  • Logistics: Supply chain management, spare parts availability, and fueling operations for extended missions.

Notable Maintenance and Training Programs

  • Advanced Aircraft Maintenance Program (AAMP) for senior technicians.
  • Logistics and Supply Chain Management Program for operational efficiency.
  • Combat and Survival Training Programs for airmen and officers.
