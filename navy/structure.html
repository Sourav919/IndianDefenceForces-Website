Organizational Structure of Indian Navy

Navy Headquarters

The Navy Headquarters in New Delhi is the apex body of the Indian Navy. The Chief of the Naval Staff (CNS) leads the Indian Navy and is supported by the Vice Chief of Naval Staff (VCNS) and the Deputy Chiefs. The headquarters also includes several specialized branches for operations, training, personnel, and logistics.

Naval Commands

  • Western Naval Command: Mumbai - Western Seaboard
  • Eastern Naval Command: Visakhapatnam - Eastern Seaboard
  • Southern Naval Command: Kochi - Southern India
  • Andaman and Nicobar Command: Port Blair - Islands Region

Functional Commands

  • Training Command: Kochi - Training of naval personnel
  • Material Command: Mumbai - Supply chain, logistics, and maintenance

Hierarchy

Officer Ranks

  • Admiral: Chief of Naval Staff, overall command of the Indian Navy
  • Vice Admiral: Senior command roles, strategic planning
  • Rear Admiral: Heads commands, divisions, or flotillas
  • Commodore: Leads a squadron or establishment

More Officer Ranks

  • Captain: Commands ships, submarines, or other large naval units
  • Commander: Commands smaller ships or submarines
  • Lieutenant Commander: Leads smaller units, assists with command responsibilities
  • Lieutenant: Junior officer, responsible for sections of ships or submarines

Seaman Ranks

  • Master Chief Petty Officer: Senior enlisted advisor
  • Chief Petty Officer: Leads and manages sailors
  • Petty Officer: Supervises and manages junior sailors
  • Leading Seaman: Junior leadership role, assisting with tasks
  • Seaman: Entry-level position, performs technical tasks
