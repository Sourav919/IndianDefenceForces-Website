Commands: Indian Army

The Indian Army is one of the largest and most powerful armies in the world, with over 1.2 million active personnel. It is divided into seven commands, each responsible for a specific region or operational area. These commands are:

Details

Northern Command - Udhampur, Jammu and Kashmir

The Northern Command is responsible for the defense of India’s northern borders with Pakistan and China. It is the largest command in the Indian Army, with over 300,000 personnel. The Northern Command also includes the Indian Army’s elite strike forces, such as the Special Frontier Force and the Parachute Regiment.

Western Command – Chandigarh

The Western Command is responsible for the defense of India’s western borders with Pakistan. It is the second-largest command in the Indian Army, with over 250,000 personnel. The Western Command also includes the Indian Army’s armored and mechanized forces.

Eastern Command

The Eastern Command is responsible for the defense of India’s eastern borders with Bangladesh, China, and Myanmar. It is the third-largest command in the Indian Army, with over 200,000 personnel. The Eastern Command also includes the Indian Army’s counter-insurgency forces.

Southern Command

The Southern Command is responsible for the defense of India’s southern borders with Sri Lanka and the Maldives. It is the fourth-largest command in the Indian Army, with over 150,000 personnel. The Southern Command also includes the Indian Army’s amphibious and aviation forces.

South-Western Command

The South-Western Command is responsible for the defense of India’s southwestern borders with Pakistan and Rajasthan. It is the fifth-largest command in the Indian Army, with over 100,000 personnel. The South-Western Command also includes the Indian Army’s desert forces.

Central Command

The Central Command is responsible for the defense of India’s central region. It is the sixth-largest command in the Indian Army, with over 50,000 personnel. The Central Command also includes the Indian Army’s rapid reaction forces.

Training Command

The Training Command is responsible for the training of all Indian Army personnel. It is the smallest command in the Indian Army, with over 25,000 personnel. The Training Command also includes the Indian Army’s military academies and training schools.
